Airline Baggage Fees Explained: Avoid Expensive Charges

📅 Published: January 15, 2025 | ⏱️ 7 minute read

Airline baggage fees are confusing and expensive. Each airline charges differently. Some charge per kg, some per bag, some by weight brackets. This guide explains exactly how much you'll pay and how to avoid these fees.

How Airlines Charge for Excess Baggage

Method 1: Per Kilogram

Most Indian airlines charge ₹500–1,000 per kg of excess weight.

Example: You have 28 kg when limit is 23 kg. You're 5 kg over. At ₹800/kg, you pay ₹4,000.

Method 2: Per Bag

Some international airlines charge a fixed fee per extra bag (not per kg).

Example: $150 per extra bag (~₹12,600). Doesn't matter if it's 10 kg or 25 kg.

Method 3: Weight Bracket

Some airlines charge different amounts for different weight ranges.

Example: 1-5 kg = ₹2,000 | 6-10 kg = ₹4,000 | 11-15 kg = ₹6,000

Hidden Baggage Fees to Know About

1. Oversized Bag Fees

If your bag exceeds size limits (even if within weight), airlines charge extra.

  • IndiGo: Oversized fees: ₹2,500–5,000
  • Air India: Up to ₹3,500

2. Sporting Equipment

Surfboards, skis, guitars count as extra bags.

  • Most airlines: $100–200 extra (~₹8,400–16,800)

3. Unbalanced Weight Distribution

If one bag is 35 kg and another is 15 kg (total 50 kg but one exceeds limits), you pay extra.

4. Cabin Baggage Overages

If your carry-on is too heavy/large:

  • Gate checking fee: ₹500–2,000
  • Forced to check at gate: Additional baggage fees apply
