Smart Packing Strategy for Students

Step 1: Separate Items Into 3 Categories

MUST CARRY (Carry-on + Checked):

  • Laptop + charger (never check electronics!)
  • Phone + chargers
  • Medications (original bottles)
  • Important documents (passport, visa, insurance)
  • Visas, travel tickets
  • Money/cards
  • Change of clothes (in case luggage delays)

SHOULD CARRY (Checked bags):

  • 5-7 tops/shirts
  • 2-3 pairs pants/trousers
  • 1 formal outfit (for presentations)
  • Underwear + socks (1 week)
  • Shoes (2 pairs: casual + formal)
  • Light jacket/sweater
  • Basic toiletries

SHIP SEPARATELY (with Beta Ji):

  • Textbooks (extremely heavy)
  • Heavy course materials
  • Extra seasonal clothes (buy locally)
  • Bedding (usually provided in dorm)
  • Heavy winter items (if moving to cold climate)

Step 2: Calculate Luggage Weight

Typical student breakdown:

  • Textbooks: 15-25 kg (SHIP THIS)
  • Clothes: 10-15 kg
  • Toiletries: 3-5 kg
  • Electronics: 3-5 kg
  • Documents/misc: 2-3 kg
  • TOTAL: 33-53 kg (8-27 kg excess)

Packing by Destination Climate

USA/Canada (Cold Climate)

  • Heavy winter coat (wear on flight)
  • Thermal underwear
  • Warm sweaters
  • Snow boots
  • Winter accessories (gloves, scarf, hat)

UK/Ireland (Rainy, Cool)

  • Waterproof jacket
  • Layers (sweaters, long sleeves)
  • Closed-toe shoes
  • Umbrella
  • Light scarf

Australia/New Zealand (Warm)

  • Light clothes
  • Summer clothing
  • Sunscreen (SPF 50+)
  • Hat and sunglasses
  • Light jacket (for AC indoors)

Money-Saving Tips for Students

Avoid Excess Baggage:

  • Ship textbooks (₹2,800-3,500)
  • Buy winter clothes on arrival (often cheaper)
  • Don't pack "just in case" items
  • Choose lightweight fabrics

Timing:

  • Ship 5-7 days before you arrive
  • Items arrive at dorm safely
  • You skip the airport baggage rush

Documentation:

  • Take photos of shipped items (insurance)
  • Keep tracking number
  • Confirm dorm delivery address
