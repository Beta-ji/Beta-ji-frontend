Wedding Lehenga Care Guide: How to Ship & Preserve Your Bridal Outfit

📅 Published: January 15, 2025 ⏱️ 8 minute read 👤 By Beta Ji Wedding Team

Your lehenga is precious. Whether it's embroidered silk, chiffon, or raw silk with zari work, it's more than clothing—it's a memory. That one day determines everything about your wedding photos.

Improper packing and handling can lead to creases, tarnished zari, embroidery damage, and fading. This guide ensures your lehenga stays beautiful—today and for generations.

The Golden Rules of Lehenga Care

  1. Never fold a lehenga - Always hang flat or lay completely flat
  2. Use acid-free tissue paper - Prevents yellowing and discoloration
  3. Store in cool, dry place - No moisture, no direct sunlight
  4. Ship separately - Never mix with other baggage
  5. Professional handling only - Not DIY ironing or pressing

Before the Wedding: Storage

Month 1–2 Before Wedding

  • Get professional dry cleaning (specify "embroidery care")
  • Let dry completely (24 hours minimum)
  • Store in breathable cotton bag (NEVER plastic)
  • Place tissue paper between all folds
  • Keep in cool, dark closet
  • NO cedar balls (they stain silk)
  • NO naphthalene (damages embroidery)

For Embroidered/Zari Lehengas

  • Wrap each section (blouse, skirt, dupatta) in acid-free tissue
  • Place in separate cotton drawstring bag
  • Store FLAT in drawer (never hang - weight damages embroidery)
  • Check every 2 weeks for pests or discoloration

How to Pack Your Lehenga for Shipping

Materials Needed

  • Acid-free tissue paper (buy online, not regular tissue)
  • Breathable cotton drawstring bag
  • Box with padding (if shipping)
  • Tissue paper (lots of it)
  • Label tape + marker (for labeling)

Packing Steps

  1. Prepare the garment: Ensure completely clean and dry
  2. Separate pieces: Keep blouse, skirt, and dupatta separate
  3. Wrap blouse:
    • Lay tissue paper flat
    • Place blouse on tissue (embroidered side up)
    • Fold tissue around it gently
    • Wrap in another layer of tissue
  4. Wrap skirt:
    • Lay skirt flat on tissue
    • DON'T fold if possible—roll gently if you must
    • Place tissue between each layer
    • Wrap completely in multiple tissue layers
  5. Wrap dupatta:
    • Fold gently into thirds
    • Wrap in tissue paper
  6. Pack in bag:
    • Place all wrapped pieces in cotton bag
    • Add silica gel packets (not cedar)
    • Don't compress—let it breathe
  7. Label clearly:
    • Your name
    • "FRAGILE - WEDDING WEAR"
    • Hotel address
    • Phone number

Day Before Wedding: Preparation

Unpack Carefully

  • Lay on clean bed (not luggage)
  • Don't touch with wet or dirty hands
  • Let breathe for 2–3 hours
  • Never use a direct iron—only gentle steam
  • If wrinkled, steam from distance (not touching fabric)

Create a Quick-Fix Kit

  • Lint roller (for dust)
  • Stain remover pen (for last-minute marks)
  • Small sewing kit (basic repairs)
  • Safety pins (for fit adjustments)
  • Extra zari thread (matching color)
  • Masking tape (for accidental tears)

Common Issues & Solutions

Problem Cause Solution Prevention
Creases Folding, improper storage Light steam (not direct iron) Store flat, use tissue paper
Tarnished zari Moisture, humidity Professional restoration (₹1,000–3,000) Keep dry, store with silica gel
Stains Spills, makeup, sweat Professional dry cleaning immediately Avoid contact with food/makeup
Loose embroidery Handling, weight Professional repair (₹500–2,000) Store flat, don't hang by embroidery
Color fading Sunlight exposure Can't reverse (prevention only) Store away from windows

During the Wedding: Protection

While Wearing

  • Wear slip underneath (protects from body oils and sweat)
  • Be careful with makeup and jewelry (can snag delicate fabric)
  • Avoid sitting in damp chairs
  • Keep away from food and drinks
  • Don't lean against walls or furniture

After Wearing (Same Night)

  • Don't fold immediately—lay flat
  • Hang on padded hanger OR lay in wardrobe
  • Spot-clean any marks (gently with damp cloth)
  • Air dry overnight
  • NEVER use plastic dry-cleaning bags

Post-Wedding: Long-Term Storage

Within 1 Week

  • Professional dry cleaning (specify embroidery care)
  • Detailed inspection for any damage
  • Let dry completely

Storage for Years

  • Fold with acid-free tissue paper between layers
  • Store in breathable cotton bag
  • Keep in cool, dark place
  • Check every 6 months for pests or discoloration
  • Store with silica gel packets (NOT cedar)
  • Keep away from windows and direct light
