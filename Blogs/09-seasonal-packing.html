Packing for India's Climate: Summer, Monsoon & Winter

📅 Published: January 15, 2025 | ⏱️ 9 minute read

India has three very different seasons. Packing for summer is completely different from packing for winter or monsoon. Pack wrong and you'll be uncomfortable, buy unnecessary clothes, or face damaged belongings.

Summer: March–May (Heat & Humidity)

Temperature Range

  • North India: 35-45°C
  • South India: 30-40°C
  • Coastal areas: High humidity (80-90%)

What to Pack

  • Light, breathable fabrics (cotton, linen)
  • Loose-fitting clothes (air circulation)
  • Sleeveless tops and short sleeves
  • Lightweight pants (khaki, cotton)
  • Light skirts/dresses (cotton)
  • Minimal undergarments (humidity makes them uncomfortable)
  • Flip-flops + light walking shoes
  • Hat or cap (sun protection)
  • Sunglasses (UV protection)
  • Sunscreen SPF 50+
  • Light scarf (for temples, sun protection)
  • Lightweight jacket (for heavy AC indoors)

Colors to Choose

  • White, cream, light pastels
  • Avoid dark colors (absorb heat)

Special Tip

Pack moisture-wicking fabrics if possible. Synthetic blends dry faster than pure cotton.

Monsoon: June–August (Rain & Humidity)

Temperature Range

  • 20-30°C (cooler than summer)
  • Humidity: 90%+ (extremely wet)
  • Heavy rainfall most days

What to Pack

  • Quick-dry fabrics (synthetic blend)
  • Closed-toe waterproof shoes (water-resistant)
  • Waterproof jacket or rain poncho
  • Umbrella (small, compact)
  • Multiple pairs of socks
  • Plastic bags (for wet clothes)
  • Hand towel (quick-dry microfiber)
  • Light sweater (for cool mornings/AC)
  • Lightweight full-length pants
  • Long sleeves (protection from wet surfaces)

Fabric Tips

  • Avoid pure cotton (takes forever to dry)
  • Choose synthetic or cotton-poly blends
  • Pack more socks than usual (they get wet)
  • Bring moisture-absorbing undergarments

Footwear

Waterproof shoes are essential. Wet streets, poor drainage in smaller cities, and sudden showers mean regular shoes get ruined.

Winter: November–February (Cool & Dry)

Temperature Range

  • North India: 5-20°C
  • South India: 15-25°C
  • Very pleasant, low humidity

What to Pack

  • Layering pieces (thermal innerwear)
  • Warm sweater or fleece jacket
  • Light jacket or cardigan
  • Long pants (jeans, chinos)
  • Closed-toe shoes
  • Scarf or shawl (for warmth + versatility)
  • Warm socks
  • Light gloves (if visiting Himalayas)
  • Hat or beanie (if very cold region)

Layering Strategy

Mornings are cold (10-15°C), afternoons are warm (20-25°C). Layer so you can adjust throughout the day.

Pro Tip

One thermal base layer under your regular clothes gives tons of warmth without bulk.

Universal Tips for All Seasons

  • Pack neutral colors: Black, navy, white, khaki, gray mix and match everything
  • Choose versatile pieces: Scarf works as shawl, beach cover, extra layer
  • Pack less: Indians shops have everything you forgot
  • Plan laundry: Most hotels offer laundry service (₹50-200 per item)
  • Bring travel-sized toiletries: Indian summers spoil sunscreen quickly

Region-Specific Adjustments

Coastal Areas (Goa, Kerala, Mumbai)

  • Higher humidity year-round
  • Summer: Expect salt water damage (bring extra sunscreen)
  • Monsoon: Very heavy rains (better waterproofing needed)

Himalayas (Shimla, Manali, Nainital)

  • Much colder than plains
  • Winter (November-March): Bring heavy winter gear
  • Summer: Still cool mornings/evenings
  • High altitude: Sun is stronger (SPF 60+ recommended)

Deserts (Rajasthan)

  • Extreme temperature swings (hot days, cold nights)
  • Pack layers even in summer
  • Winter evenings can drop to 5°C

What to Skip (No Matter the Season)

  • ❌ Multiple pairs of heavy shoes
  • ❌ Formal dress clothes (unless business trip)
  • ❌ Heavy winter coat (Indian cold is manageable with layers)
  • ❌ Brand-name cosmetics (available everywhere, cheaper)
  • ❌ Heavy books (buy locally or use e-reader)
